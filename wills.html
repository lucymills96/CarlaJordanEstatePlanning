Why Make a Will

Many people delay making a Will because it’s difficult to think about what happens after we’re gone. But life changes such as a new baby, a serious illness, a relationship change, or buying a home are reminders of how important it is to plan ahead.

Creating a Will gives you control and clarity. It ensures your assets are passed on in line with your wishes and helps reduce stress and uncertainty for your loved ones. A professionally written Will gives you control, clarity, and peace of mind.

With a Will, you can:

  • Clearly express who should receive your assets, savings, and belongings.
  • Appoint guardians for your children in the event of your passing.
  • Protect unmarried partners or stepchildren who might otherwise be excluded.
  • Safeguard sentimental or valuable items for named individuals.
  • Specify funeral wishes.
  • Reduce inheritance tax where possible through proper planning.
  • Provide for vulnerable or disabled loved ones without affecting their benefits.
  • Make charitable donations as part of your legacy.

What Happens If You Don’t Have a Will?

Without a Will, your estate is distributed under the laws of intestacy, which may not reflect your wishes. This can result in:

  • Partners receiving nothing if you’re not married or in a civil partnership.
  • Children potentially not being cared for by the people you would have chosen.
  • Family disputes, stress, and delays in distributing your estate.
  • Higher tax liabilities and missed planning opportunities.
  • Loved ones being unintentionally excluded.

Things To Consider

  • Executors

    Name the people you want to take charge and administer your estate.

  • Trustees

    Name who should be responsible to look after money that is due for your children when they are older.

  • Guardians

    Record who would be responsible for looking after your children should the worst happen.

  • Beneficiaries

    Identify who will benefit and receive your possessions, money and property.
