Cookie Policy

Cookie Policy for Carla Jordan Estate Planning

Effective Date: June 2025

At Carla Jordan Estate Planning, we are committed to being transparent about how we use cookies and the data they collect. This Cookie Policy explains what cookies are, how and why we use them, and how you can control or manage them through your browser settings.

What Are Cookies?

A cookie is a small text file that is placed and stored on your computer, smartphone, tablet, or other device when you visit a website. Cookies are widely used to make websites function properly, to improve their efficiency, and to provide useful information to website owners about user behaviour. For more information about cookies, visit aboutcookies.org or allaboutcookies.org.

Why We Use Cookies

Cookies help us improve your experience on our website. They allow users to navigate the site more easily and enable certain core functions. Cookies may also be used to gather anonymous statistics, which help us understand how our website is used and how we can improve it.

We use cookies for purposes including:

  • Enabling you to complete tasks such as online quote requests (session cookie)
  • Maintaining secure browsing sessions for your protection (session cookie)
  • Enhancing functionality and personalising your user experience
  • Tracking website traffic and user behaviour (persistent cookies via Google Analytics)

Importantly, the cookies used on our site do not contain any personal or sensitive data and cannot be used to identify you personally.

Types of Cookies We Use

  • Session Cookies: Temporary cookies that are deleted when you close your browser. They help with functions like secure logins and navigation continuity.
  • Persistent Cookies: Remain on your device until they expire or are deleted. They help us recognise returning visitors, remember preferences, and collect analytics data.

Specific Cookies We Use

Cookie Name Purpose
ASP.NET_SESSIONID This session cookie is necessary for the operation of our online quote engine. It allows data to be passed securely between pages.
utma (Google Analytics) Tracks how many times a visitor has been to the site, and when their first and last visits occurred.
utmb and utmc (Google Analytics) Work together to calculate how long a visit lasts. utmb logs the time of entry, and utmc logs when the visitor leaves.
utmz (Google Analytics) Tracks where the visitor came from (e.g. search engine, link, keyword) and the visitor's location.

All Google Analytics cookies collect aggregated and anonymous data to help us analyse trends and improve the structure and performance of our website. These cookies do not collect personally identifiable information.

Managing, Disabling and Enabling Cookies

You have the right to choose whether to accept or refuse cookies. Most browsers accept cookies by default, but you can change your settings to block or delete them.

Visit aboutcookies.org or allaboutcookies.org for guidance on controlling cookies in your browser.

To opt out of Google Analytics tracking across all websites, use Google's opt-out tool: http://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout

ICO Cookie Directive Compliance

We are actively working towards full compliance with the ICO Cookie Directive (EU ePrivacy Directive). We use cookies responsibly to ensure optimal website functionality and improve services. We also respect the "Do Not Track" (DNT) setting where technically feasible.
